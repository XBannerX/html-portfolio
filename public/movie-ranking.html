The Best Movies According to Corey

My top 3 movies of all-time.

Forest Gump

Forrest Gump is a romantic comedy-drama film that chronicles several decades of American history through the perspective of its simple-minded, kind-hearted titular character from Alabama. Though he has an IQ of 75 and wore leg braces as a child, Forrest's supportive mother teaches him that he is no different from anyone else, and he inadvertently stumbles into numerous historical events and extraordinary achievements.

Aladdin

The story of Aladdin generally follows a kind-hearted but impoverished young man who discovers a magic lamp containing a powerful, wish-granting Genie. With the Genie's help, Aladdin transforms himself into a wealthy prince to woo the beautiful Princess Jasmine, whom he met in the marketplace. However, the wicked royal advisor, Jafar, seeks the lamp for himself to seize power and overthrow the Sultan. Through cleverness and bravery, Aladdin thwarts Jafar's evil schemes, eventually tricking the villain into becoming a genie himself and trapping him in a lamp. In the end, Aladdin chooses to free his friend the Genie with his final wish, proving his noble character; the Sultan then changes the law to allow the princess to marry for love, and Aladdin and Jasmine live happily ever after.

The Lion King

The Lion King is an animated musical epic that follows the journey of a young lion cub named Simba, who is the heir to the throne of the Pride Lands. His evil uncle, Scar, murders Simba's father, King Mufasa, and manipulates Simba into believing he is responsible for the death, forcing the young cub to flee into exile. In the jungle, Simba befriends the carefree duo Timon and Pumbaa and lives a life free of responsibility. Years later, his childhood friend Nala finds him and urges him to return to reclaim his rightful place as king from Scar's tyrannical rule. Simba ultimately accepts his destiny, returns to the Pride Lands, defeats Scar in battle, and restores balance to his kingdom.
